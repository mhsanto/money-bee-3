Navigating Home Loans & Property Financing in Malaysia

Buying a home is the biggest financial commitment most Malaysians will ever make. Securing the right "pinjaman perumahan" (housing loan) is just as important as choosing the right property. This guide breaks down the basics.

What is a Home Loan?

A home loan (or property financing) is a long-term loan used to purchase a residential property. Unlike a personal loan, a home loan is "secured," meaning the property itself is the collateral. If you fail to pay, the bank has the right to repossess the property.

Key Terms to Know:

  • • Margin of Finance (MOF): This is the percentage of the property's price that the bank will lend you. For your first or second home, this is typically 90%. This means you must pay the other 10% as a down payment in cash.
  • • Lock-in Period: A period (e.g., 3-5 years) where you will be charged a high penalty fee if you pay off the loan early (e.g., by selling the house or refinancing).
  • • MRTA vs. MLTA (Mortgage Insurance):
    • MRTA (Mortgage Reducing Term Assurance): A cheaper insurance policy that pays off the remaining loan balance if you die or suffer total permanent disability. The coverage amount reduces as your loan reduces.
    • MLTA (Mortgage Level Term Assurance): A more expensive policy that pays out a fixed cash sum if you die or suffer TPD. This cash goes to your family, who can then choose to pay off the loan or use the money for other needs.

Flexi vs. Semi-Flexi vs. Term Loan 

This is the most important choice you'll make:

  1. a.Term Loan (Basic): A standard loan with a fixed payment schedule. You cannot easily pay extra to reduce interest, and you cannot withdraw any extra money you've paid. (Least flexible).
  2. b.Semi-Flexi Loan: This is the most common. You can pay extra money at any time to reduce your principal and save on interest. You can also withdraw that extra money, but it usually involves a small fee and some paperwork.
  3. c.Full-Flexi Loan (Flexi): This links your loan account to a current account. Any extra money you put in the current account automatically offsets your loan principal, saving you interest. You can withdraw this money anytime with no fees. (Most flexible, but may have a small monthly fee).

Choosing the right home loan is a 30-year decision. Our platform helps you get the best rates from multiple banks, and our human experts can explain the difference between a "Flexi" and "Semi-Flexi" loan in simple terms.

Eligibility

General requirements for debt consolidation in Malaysia

Citizenship

  • Malaysian citizen or Malaysian Permanent Resident.

Age:

  • 21 – 60 years old.

Minimum income

  • Salaried, self-employed, or business income acceptable with proof.

Margin of Finance (MoF)

  • Up to 90% for first homebuyers (10% down payment required).

Documents required:

  • MyKad / IC
  • Salary slips / bank statements / EPF statements (3–6 months)
  • Property valuation report (if secondary market property)
  • Credit Record: CCRIS / CTOS report will be reviewed.
  • For Islamic Financing: Property must not be used for activities against Shariah.
